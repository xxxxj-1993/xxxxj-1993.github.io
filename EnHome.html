About me

I was born in 1993. I received the bachelor's degree, the Master's degree and the Docter's degrees from Nanchang University in 2014, 2017 and 2020, respectively. My research focus include the magneto-optical (MO) materials-based one-way waveguide and its applications in (ultra-) subwavelength functional devices. To date, I have reported several MO one-way waveguide in which the completely one-way propagating waves were achieved. Moreover, relying on the one-way structures, I have achieved the optical functionalities such as optical filter, switch, buffer and field enhancement. I study not only the microwave EM wave but also the rapidly developing terahertz waves. Besides, I am also interested in the index-near-zero modes/materials and all-optical logic operations.

Research Focus

  • MO one-way waveguide：The EM waves in the MO systems always exibit the nonreciprocal propagation characteristics because of the existence of the external magnetic field or magnetization. Using such nonreciprocity, I have designed several one-way structures which were able to be used in achieving special optical functional devices such as trapping and releasing the EM waves, optical switch and buffer.

  • INZ theory and its applications：Index-near-zero (INZ) modes are widely studied in the epsilon-near-zero (ENZ) materials. Due to the near-zero phase shift, the INZ modes are suitable for applications like optical cloaking.

  • All-optical logic operations：The electric logic operations are suffering from the dramatically enhanced crosstalk and energy loss in the microcomputers. The optical computation based on the all-optical logic gates are believed to be a potential solution.
